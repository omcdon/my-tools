Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is an IP address? — An IP (Internet Protocol) address is a unique identifier assigned to your device on a network. IPv4 addresses look like 192.168.1.1, while IPv6 addresses are longer hexadecimal strings.
  • Is my location accurate? — IP geolocation provides an approximate location (usually city-level). It is not as accurate as GPS and may be off by several miles, especially for mobile connections.
  • Does a VPN change my detected IP? — Yes. If you use a VPN, this tool will show the VPN server's IP and location, not your actual ones.
  • Can websites see my IP? — Yes. Every website you visit can see your public IP address. It is a fundamental part of how internet communication works.
  • What is the difference between public and private IP? — Your public IP is assigned by your ISP and visible to the internet. Your private IP (like 192.168.x.x) is used within your local network and not visible externally.
