How to Use

  • Drag and drop images into the drop zone, or click to browse your files.
  • Select your target format: WebP (smallest file size, recommended for web), JPG (universal compatibility), or PNG (lossless quality).
  • Adjust the quality slider (1–100%). Higher quality means larger files. 80% is a good balance for web images.
  • Click "Convert All" to process all uploaded images at once.
  • Download individual files or click "Download All (ZIP)" to get everything as a ZIP archive.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Is my data uploaded to a server? No. All image conversion happens locally in your browser using the Canvas API. Your images never leave your device.
  • What is WebP and why should I use it? WebP is a modern image format developed by Google. It typically produces files 25–35% smaller than JPEG at equivalent quality, making it ideal for websites.
  • What's the maximum file size? Each file can be up to 50MB. For batch processing, we recommend converting 10–20 images at a time for best performance.
  • Will converting to JPG lose quality? Yes, JPG is a lossy format. If you need pixel-perfect quality, choose PNG. For web use, JPG or WebP at 80% quality is usually sufficient.
  • Does this tool support batch conversion? Yes. Drop or select multiple images and they will all be converted at once. You can download them individually or as a ZIP.
