How This Calculator Works

Calculation Method

This calculator estimates your total tax liability as a 1099 freelancer by computing three components:

  1. Federal Income Tax — Calculated using 2026 progressive tax brackets applied to your taxable income after all deductions.
  2. Self-Employment Tax — 15.3% applied to 92.35% of your net earnings (Social Security + Medicare).
  3. State Income Tax — A simplified effective rate applied to your net income.

Self-Employment Tax Details

As a freelancer, you pay both the employer and employee portions of Social Security and Medicare taxes. The self-employment tax is calculated on 92.35% of your net earnings (the other 7.65% is already excluded):

  • Social Security: 12.4% on earnings up to the $176,100 wage base (2026 estimate)
  • Medicare: 2.9% on all earnings (no cap)

You can deduct half of your self-employment tax (the “employer portion”) from your taxable income, which reduces your federal income tax.

Qualified Business Income (QBI) Deduction

The calculator applies a simplified QBI deduction of 20% of your net business income. In practice, the QBI deduction has income thresholds and limitations based on your taxable income and the type of business. This calculator uses the maximum 20% rate as a simplified estimate.

State Tax Estimation

State taxes are estimated using a simplified effective rate rather than calculating each state's unique bracket system. The rates represent approximate effective rates for a typical freelancer income. Some states use a flat rate, others have progressive brackets. For precise state tax calculations, consult your state's tax authority.
