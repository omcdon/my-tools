Frequently Asked Questions

How do you calculate meeting cost?

We use the formula: Total Cost = (Number of Attendees × Average Annual Cost ÷ 2,080) × Meeting Duration in Hours. The 2,080 figure represents standard working hours per year (40 hours/week × 52 weeks).

What is fully-loaded employee cost?

It’s the total cost to employ someone, including gross salary, health insurance, retirement contributions, payroll taxes, paid leave, office space, equipment, and other overhead. A common shortcut: multiply gross salary by 1.3–1.4.

Is my data private?

Absolutely. All calculations happen entirely in your browser. Nothing is sent to any server, stored in any database, or tracked. The page even works offline after the first load.

Why should I care about meeting cost?

The average professional spends 23 hours per week in meetings, and many of them are unproductive. Understanding the real dollar cost helps teams make better decisions about when to schedule meetings vs. send an email or use async communication.
